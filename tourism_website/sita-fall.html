Sita Fall :- Though it is one of the lesser known waterfalls, your trip of the waterfalls in Ranchi is incomplete without a visit here. Being close to Jonha, it is not difficult to reach here. The rocky gradient down where the water trickles, seems to have been sculpted by none other than Nature itself. The view caresses away both your physical and mental strains, leaving you recharged as the fresh and foaming water of the falls. The site is well connected with both rail and bus routes. Nestled in a tranquil setting surrounded by dense forests, Sita Falls offers a picturesque and calming retreat. The waterfall descends from a height of around 144 feet and forms a natural pool below, presenting a breathtaking view. With rocks and boulders encircling the pool, it’s an ideal location to unwind and relish the natural beauty of the surroundings.

If you are capable and energized to hike more, do not miss the opportunity to trek over the boulders and reach the base of the waterfall. It’s a very short hiking path but offers unforgettable experiences. The trail involves traversing over uneven boulders and crossing multiple narrow streams that are initiated from the falls. The hike may be slightly strenuous for the elderly and children, but the spectacular frontal view of the waterfall at the end of the trek makes it an incredibly rewarding experience. Upon reaching Sita Falls, you’ll discover a peaceful ambiance, unlike the bustling crowds that frequent other popular Ranchi attractions such as Dassam Waterfalls, Hundru Falls, or Patratu. The lack of awareness about Sita Falls amongst tourists is one of the reasons for its relatively unoccupied nature.
